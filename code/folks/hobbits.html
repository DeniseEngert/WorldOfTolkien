Hobbits

General

ancient, unintrusive people
relatives of Men more than of Elves and Dwarves
spoke the language of Men (of their own fashion) and liked and disliked much the same things Men did
beginnings lie far back in the elder days, now lost and forgotten

Appearances

Hobbits are small people, about half the height of humans, even smaller than dwarves (between two and four feet).
They have no beards, but thick, brown, curly hair. They are inclined to be a bit fat around the stomach.
Their faces are good-natured and they laugh a deep, fruity laugh, especially after dinner (which they have twice a day, when they can get it). Broad, bright eyes, red-cheeked, with mouths apt to laughter, and eating, and drinking.
The colours of their clothes are bright, chiefly green and yellow. They wear no shoes, though, because their feet developed leathery soles and thick, warm, brown hair.

Skills

They hear well, have the ability to move very silently and they possess the art of disappearing swiftly.
Hobbits are quick of hearing and sharp-eyed.
They have long and very skillful fingers, additionally to being very good with tools.
Hobbits know little to no magic.

Nature

loved to eat and drink and laugh - often and heartily (six meals a day)
hospitable and delighted in parties and presents, which they gave away freely and eagerly accepted
do not hurry unnecessary, but are nimble and deft in movements
love peace and quiet and a well-tilled earth

Tribes, Families, Breeds

Breeds

Harfoots:
  • browner in skin, smaller and shorter, beardless and bootless,
  • preferred highlands and hillsides
  • had much to do with dwarves in ancient times
  • lived long in the foothills of the mountains, moved westward early,
  • most inclined to settle in one place, preserved their ancestral habit of living in tunnels and holes the longest
Stoors:
  • broader and heavier in build, hands and feeds where larger,
  • preferred flatlands and riversides,
  • came west after the Harfoots
Fallohides:
  • fairer in skin and hair, taller and slimmer than the others,
  • lovers of trees and woodlands,
  • least in numbers,
  • northerly branch,
  • more skill in language and song than in handicrafts

The Hobbit - There And Back Again

An Unexpected Journey

In his 50th year - S. R. 1341 - Bilbo is chosen by Gandalf to accompany Thorin's fellowship on its quest to take back Erebor.
The unexpected journey is preceded by an unexpected house party, where Bilbo learns about the rough but good-natured personalities that will be his company in the coming months - and adventures.
He also learns about the terms and conditions of the quest, but doesn't seem to grasp the gravity of it, yet.

Roast Mutton

At the end of April, after a short-lived belief the danger of going on an adventure has passed by, Bilbo and the dwarves start their journey in Bywater.
During a particularly unpleasant patch of the journey in May, Bilbo got his first job as a burglar and went up against trolls, got the dwarves in trouble and Gandalf saved their asses.

A Short Rest

In June, our fellowship reaches Rivendell, where they stayed for at least a fortnight. Nothing remarkable is to say about that stay. They got good sleep and good food.
The day after midsummer eve they traveled on, but not before Elrond discovered moon runes on Thorin's map that told them when and how they can find the secret entry to Erebor.

Over The Hill And Under The Hill

The journey let from Rivendell directly into the Misty Mountains, where after a few days of constant climbing Bilbo and the Dwarves witnessed a battle of the Rock Giants, fled from it - on a Monday night or a Tuesday morning - right into the arms of Goblins.

Riddles In The Dark

Fleeing from the goblins, Bilbo is separated from his companions and finds himself alone in a cave, where he meets an important turning point of his life. And Gollum.
A game of riddles leads to another flight and the discovery of something ... very helpful.

Out Of The Frying-Pan Into The Fire

Bilbo got out of the Goblin Caves and found his friends in the woods. By now it's Thursday, and the fellowship has gone astray too far to the north and an awkward country lays ahead.
They witness an assembly of wargs and become aware of a planned undertaking to kill the people of the woods. A battle of fire between the companionship, the wargs and goblins flares up
and only air support is able to help them out of this pickle.

Queer Loggings

Gandalf, Bilbo and the dwarfs find shelter for two nights in a house most remarkable, with an equally remarkable host.
Stories are exchanged, help, warnings and good advice is granted. They leave Beorn's house and ride north along the Great River for four days until they reach the edge of Mirkwood.
This is also where Gandalf leaves the tour party and goes on wizard business.

Flies And Spiders

The dwarfs and the hobbit enter the dark forrest and experience a dreadful journey through darkness. After many days they reach the enchanted river, manage to cross it and by accident Bombur becomes a very inactive member of the troop. Four days after the crossed the water, they sense first signs that they are getting close to a civilisation again. Two days later at least Bilbo sees the sun again by being sent up into the trees to check how far they still have to go until they reached the other side of the forrest. Hungry and thirsty they witness a gathering of Elves, and in the hopes of food and drink, they leave the path ... and lose their way. They are attacked by giant Spiders, are saved by Bilbo and his very useful find. And they discover too late that Thorin is missing.

Barrels Out Of Bond

One day later the remaining dwarves and Bilbo chose a direction and try to find the way out of the wood. But they are apprehended by the elves and imprisoned in the Elvenking's underground castle. Only Bilbo escapes with the help of the ring, but gets into the castle anyway, where he searches for a way to help the dwarves for about two weeks. The solution were barrels, in which they floated out of the Wood Elves kingdom towards Lake Town.

A Warm Welcome

There he is: The Lonely Mountain. Bilbo sees it for the very first time ... and doesn't like the look of it.
The dwarves are welcomed in Lake Town and stay for about two weeks. They recover from there ordeal in the prison cells of the King of the Wood Elves and their wet escape. Provided with weapons, provisions, ponies and horses, they set out on the last leg of their journey.

On The Doorstep

For three days they paddled upstream through the Long Lake to reach the place where the people of Lake Town brought the horses and ponies.
They find, after some exploring, the secret door into Erebor, but it takes them until the last week of autumn to find at long last the secret keyhole and are able to open the door.

Inside Information

The burglar does what he is hired for, and wakes the dragon. It becomes very clear that the plan of taking back Erebor has a glaring hole: how to deal with the dragon.
Smaug is enraged given Bilbo's theft and the presence of dwarves, and after failing to find them, he sets out to get revenge on the people Lake Town.

Not At Home

For two days after Smaug left Erebor the dwarves and Bilbo hide in and wander through its halls. They find its treasures, weapons, its old greatness and are more than determined to take back what is theirs. Bilbo finds the Arkenstone, and keeps that a secret

Fire And Water

Smaug takes revenge on Lake Town, but is met with resistance and by Bard, the archer, who kills the dragon with the black arrow. Smaug is destroyed, but so is Lake Town, and the survivors turn their thoughts towards Dale, and riches under the mountain.
Only three days after Smaug's demise the army of the Elvenking is already on its way towards Erebor. Two days later they arrive at Lake Town and aid the people of Lake Town. This being done, they set out towards Erebor, and eleven days after the demise of Smaug they cross the borders into the desolated land.

The Gathering Of The Clouds

We are back at Erebor three days after Smaug fell. From the ravens they learn about the demise of Smaug, but also that the people of Lake Town and the Woodelves are marching towards Erebor in hopes to retrieve the riches of the dwarves. Thorin sends for help, meanwhile he orders the fortification of Erebor. The Elves and people of the lake arrive about a week later. Negotiations with a delegation of the army at the gates of Erebor go very wrong, and Erebor is now besieged.

A Thief In The Night

News arrive that Dain and his army are only two days away. In despair given what is about to come, Bilbo sneaks out of Erebor and brings the Arkenstone to Bard and the Elvenking. And he meets again an old acquaintance: Gandalf.

The Clouds Burst

Thorin breaks with Bilbo after he confessed to have given the Arkenstone to Bard and the Elvenking. Dain's army arrives and the dwarves plans become apparent. But they are torpedoed by the arrival of another army: Bolg's Orks from the north reach plains of Erebor.
A battle erupts: The Battle Of The Five Armies. On one side, there are the Orks and the Wargs, on the other side the Elves, Men and the Dwarves.

The Return Journey

The battle is fought and won. But Thorin Oakenshield and his nephews Kili and Fili fell. They are buried deep in the mountain.
Dain becomes new King Under The Mountain and honors the arrangements with men and elves.
For Bilbo it is now time to return home. By midwinter, he, together with Gandalf and Beorn got around Mirkwood, and they spend Yuletide in Beorn's home. They stayed till spring.

The Last Stage

Gandalf and Bilbo have a second stay at Elrond's house, where they stay for a week. By now it's May. The last leg of the journey lasts into June. On June 22, S. R. 1342, they arrive at Bilbo's home in the middle of an auction, where Bilbo's belongings where sold off because he was deemed dead.

The Lord Of The Rings - The Fellowship Of The Ring

A Long Expected Party

This story begins 60 years after Bilbo started his first journey. It is now S. R. 1401, and Bilbo is about to celebrate his hundred-and-eleventh birthday, together with Frodo, who turns 33.
So, there is a party again, though not an unexpected one, where Bilbo performs a disappearing act. Frodo becomes the heir of Bag End and has to fend off relatives and friends, so they do not rob him dry. After all of Bilbo's wishes are carried out, peace returns to Bag End, but not before Gandalf swears Frodo to absolute secrecy about The Ring.

The Shadow Of The Past

Time goes by in the shire, things settle down and Bilbo becomes the subject of tales and rumours, and Frodo gains a reputation not unlike the one Bilbo acquired.
17 years after the big farewell party - it is April now - Gandalf reappears in Bag End. He tells Frodo everything he has learned about The Ring in Frodo's possession, about how he came to be there, about its former owners, about its origins and about its powers. Frodo is overwhelmed. But decisions need to be made. And a decision is being made: to leave the Shire and take The Ring with him. But he is not to go alone: Sam Gamgee has been conscripted to go with him.

Three Is Company

Three weeks went by and no further plans fo leaving have been made. Gandalf raises the pressure until a plan was formed. At the end of June Gandalf is leaving.
On September the 20th Frodo's belongings are moved to his new home. But when Gandalf still isn't back on the 22nd, a Thursday, worries arise.
Frodo, Sam and Pippin are on their way by foot, their journey will take them three days. On their way they encounter the enemy - Black Riders - for the first time and get protection and advice from High Elves for a part of their journey.

A Short Cut To Mushrooms

The next morning the Hobbits wake up and find the Elves already gone. So of they go across the country, avoiding the street and hoping to find a good shortcut. But they are followed by the Black Rider. They find shelter (and good food) with Farmer Maggot, who tells them about being questioned by a Black Rider. The good man offers to give them a ride to the Bucklebury ferry, where they are met by Merry.

A Conspiracy Unmasked

After they crossed the Brandywine they march on through the night until they finally reach Crickhollow and Frodo's new house.
And it is here where Frodo reveals his plan to leave the Shire, a plan he thought was a well kept a secret. Not only is he mistaken here, he learns, but also do his friends reveal their own secrets. And plans. And suddenly, a traveling party of two grew to four.
After this is setted, preparations are made to leave Crickhollow the next morning.

The Old Forest

In the morning the group of four set out to leave Crickhollow. Through the High Hay they finally leave the Shire and ride into the Old Forest. Fatty Bolger stays behind and works to obfuscate the departure of Frodo.
After a rather uneventful journey through the wood they encounter a rather unusual attack: lulled into sleep by a willow tree. They are saved by Tom Bombadil, who then offers them shelter for the night.

In The House Of Tom Bombadil

For two very peaceful nights the Hobbits are guests at the house of Tom Bombadil and Goldberry. They hear a lot of tales, learn a lot about affairs old and new, about beasts and beings, and the get valuable advice from Tom.

Fog On The Barrow-Downs

The next morning they say their goodbyes and take off towards Bree. But they do not get very far and come under attack of the Barrow-wights. Frodo remembers a song Tom Bombadil taught them to sing if in distress. So he sings, and Tom comes to save the day.
To secure their save journey through his land, Tom accompanies them until the reach the border of his land. Frodo recons they lost only two days by his "shortcut". By nightfall of the second day they finally reach Bree.

At The Sign Of The Prancing Pony

At last they arrive in Bree and at the Prancing Pony. After a warm welcome Frodo makes the acquaintance of Strider and performs - accidentally - a vanishing act, which disturbs the peace of the party at the Prancing Pony.

Strider

Strider and the four Hobbits have a Get-To-Know-Each-Other, after which Frodo & Co. have a new ally. But bad news loom over them. Gandalf's whereabouts are unknown, the Black Riders are in Bree, and traitors are already in their services. Precautions need to be taken.

A Knife In The Dark

The Black Riders make their first move: first in Crickhollow, but Fatty Bolger is watchful, and in the same night in Bree, and here Strider is watchful. The attack and its aftermath delay their departure for at least three hours, they can not slip away quietly anymore, and they lose money to the traitor Lutz Farning. But depart they do, and Strider leads them through the wilderness safely and mostly unobserved.
On the 5th of October, six days after they left Bree, they reached the mountains close to Weathertop, and the next day they reach Weathertop itself. The find signs that someone was here about three ays ago, but have a hard time to interpret them.

And it is here that the black riders make their next move and attack. Frodo is hit by a weapon made in Mordor.

Flight To The Ford

The group flees Weathertop, Frodo is unable to carry anything, not even himself. They flee through the woods and after six day reach the road again. They cross the bridge over Hoarwell unharmed and meet Glorfindel on their way to the Loudwater. From him they learn there is still no news about Gandalf. They continue towards the Ford of Bruinen, where they finally encounter the nine black riders again. Frodo flees over the river, makes it barely across but witnesses the defeat of the riders by elbish magic.
On the night of October the 20th, Frodo reached Rivendell.

Many Meetings

On the morning of October the 24th, at 10 o'clock, Frodo woke up in the House of Elrond and was greeted by Gandalf. Elrond healed the wound caused by the Morgul blade
A feast is held to welcome the new arrivals, where Frodo encounters Elrond for the first time, and meets Gloin.
News are exchanged, songs are sung, and finally Frodo meets Bilbo again.

The Council Of Elrond

Many guests have arrived at Rivendell, seeking council for how to deal with ongoings throughout Middle-Earth. A Meeting is called to address them and distribute news to all: Elves, Dwarves, Men. And every group had their own stories to tell: the disappearance of Balin, the escape of Gollum, the pressure on Gondor, the captivity of Gandalf, the journey of The Ring.
Discussed are all the options on hand, and most of them are dismissed. Only one option seems to be feasible: The Ring needs to be destroyed. This is the hour of the Shire-folks.

The Ring Goes South

In December the scouts - send out after the Council - return to Rivendell. Neither the Black Riders nor Gollum has been seen anywhere.
It is decided that The Ring must leave as soon as possible, and for this a Fellowship is chosen to accompany Frodo, Sam and Gandalf. For the elves Legolas is chosen, the dwarves send Gimli. For men Aragorn will join, and also Boromir, since both want to go to Gondor. Finally, Pippin and Merry insist on joining the fellowship. And so it is decided.
On an evening late in December, the Fellowship Of The Ring sets of from Rivendell. The crossed soon the border to Hollin and moved towards the Misty Mountains, where they planned to take the Redhorn Pass at Mount Caradhras. It is here where they suffer their first defeat, and they are forced turn back on January the 13th.

A Journey In The Dark

The decision is made to go through the Mines of Moria. On their journey their they are attacked a second time, this time by wolves. After their defeat, the journey continues through a landscape that seems to be changed much, and they find their way blocked even by a new lake.
But the reach the Door of Durin, through which they want to enter Moria. Their way is blocked again, this time by a missing password to open the door. Hidden in plain sight, the password is spoken, the door opens and at this exact time the companionship suffers a third attack, this time from the unnatural lake.
They manage to flee into Moria, but now find their way back out blocked.
The long journey through Moria begins and after a long while they find the Great Halls of Moria. Two nights they spend in the mines. Then they find the tomb of Balin.

The Bridge Of Khazad-Dûm

In Balin's Tomb they learn about the faith of his company. Five years they were able to hold Moria, but then were overcome by the enemy. Non of Balin's company survived.
And the fellowship is in danger to suffer the same faith, since they are attacked by Orcs and Uruks ... and something unknown to them, even Gandalf. They flee towards the Bridge of Khazad-Dûm.
They reach the bridge, but so does the enemy. And now they know what the Unknown is: a Balrog, also known as Durins Bane. Nobody but Gandalf take on the challenge. The fight is strength-sapping, but Gandalf holds the upper hand. But with a last lunge the Balrog lashes out and takes Gandalf with him into the abyss.
With the last of their strength the rest of the company reaches the gates and escapes Moria.

Lothlórien

Having escaped Moria and its new inhabitants, the company flees toward the woods of Lothlórien. They meet the Galadhrim there and are offered protection for the night. But not only the company reached Lothlórien. So did a company of orks and Gollum. The former will never leave Lorien again.

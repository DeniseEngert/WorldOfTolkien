The Lord Of The Rings - The Fellowship Of The Ring

A Long Expected Party

This story begins 60 years after Bilbo started his first journey. It is now S. R. 1401, and Bilbo is about to celebrate his hundred-and-eleventh birthday, together with Frodo, who turns 33.
So, there is a party again, though not an unexpected one, where Bilbo performs a disappearing act. Frodo becomes the heir of Bag End and has to fend off relatives and friends, so they do not rob him dry. After all of Bilbo's wishes are carried out, peace returns to Bag End, but not before Gandalf swears Frodo to absolute secrecy about The Ring.

The Shadow Of The Past

Time goes by in the shire, things settle down and Bilbo becomes the subject of tales and rumours, and Frodo gains a reputation not unlike the one Bilbo acquired.
17 years after the big farewell party - it is April now - Gandalf reappears in Bag End. He tells Frodo everything he has learned about The Ring in Frodo's possession, about how he came to be there, about its former owners, about its origins and about its powers. Frodo is overwhelmed. But decisions need to be made. And a decision is being made: to leave the Shire and take The Ring with him. But he is not to go alone: Sam Gamgee has been conscripted to go with him.

Three Is Company

Three weeks went by and no further plans fo leaving have been made. Gandalf raises the pressure until a plan was formed. At the end of June Gandalf is leaving.
On September the 20th Frodo's belongings are moved to his new home. But when Gandalf still isn't back on the 22th, a Thursday, worries arise.
Frodo, Sam and Pippin are on their way by foot, their journey will take them three days. On their way they encounter the enemy - Black Riders - for the first time and get protection and advice from High Elves for a part of their journey.

A Short Cut To Mushrooms

The next morning the Hobbits wake up and find the Elves already gone. So of they go across the country, avoiding the street and hoping to find a good shortcut. But they are followed by the Black Rider. They find shelter (and good food) with Farmer Maggot, who tells them about being questioned by a Black Rider. The good man offers to give them a ride to the Bucklebury ferry, where they are met by Merry.
